Attractions

Brookside Gardens

In Silver Spring, Maryland, there is a public garden called Brookside Gardens. The 50-acre garden has several themed gardens, including a rose garden, a butterfly garden, a scent garden, and a Japanese-style garden. The Woodland Walk, a naturalistic woodland area with paths, streams, and ponds, is another attraction open to visitors. The garden also provides educational programs, tours, and special events all year long. Although some events could have a fee, Brookside Gardens is free to enter.

Silver Spring Library

The Silver Spring Library is a modern public library located in Silver Spring, Maryland. It features a variety of amenities and services, including a large collection of books, DVDs, and other materials, free Wi-Fi, public computers, meeting rooms, and a children's play area. The library also hosts regular events and programs for all ages, such as book clubs, author talks, storytimes, and workshops. The building's architecture is designed to be energy-efficient and sustainable, featuring a green roof and solar panels. Overall, the Silver Spring Library is a valuable resource for the community, offering access to information, entertainment, and educational opportunities.

National Museum of Health and Medicine

The history of medicine and its effects on society are the focus of the Silver Spring, Maryland-based National Museum of Health and Medicine. The Defense Health Agency's museum features displays about medical research, surgical advancements, and the development of military medicine. The collection of medical artifacts, which includes preserved human specimens, medical equipment, and photographs, is open to visitors. Moreover, the museum hosts lectures, educational activities, and medical-related events. For individuals interested in the history of health, the National Museum of Health and Medicine is a must-visit location since it offers a distinctive perspective on the development of medicine and its effects on society.
